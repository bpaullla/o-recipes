Elderberry syrup

Description

Who doesn't love elderberry syrup? We people of the Balkans sure do. I have yet to meet a household, especially a rural one, where the kids do not know or love elderberry. Some even eat it fried. Can you imagine what fried flowers could taste like?

Enough chit-chat. I'm also supposed to let you know about the actual properties of this syrup. Well, it's sweet. It's best served with water, some prefer sparkling over regular. Some serve it in lemonade. It also has some health benefits that I know absolutely nothing about but I'm sure you can Google it.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups water
  • 1 cup fresh elderberries
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger root
  • 1 cup honey

Steps

  1. Combine water, elderberries, ginger, cinnamon stick, and cloves in a saucepan over medium heat; bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. Mash berries with a potato mixture to release juices. Strain the mixture through a fine sieve, retaining juice and discarding pulp, cinnamon stick, and cloves. Let cool for 30 minutes.
  3. Stir in honey until thoroughly combined.
